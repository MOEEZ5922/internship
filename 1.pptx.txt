A different approach to using Digital Biomarkers
Why they want Digital Biomarkers:
To fill the gap between CPAP adherence and actual treatment effectiveness by integrating DB.
To enable real-time, personalized interventions based on objective biomarker-driven evidence.
To improve decision trees with continuous monitoring.
Clinical data: Patient history, diagnostic reports, treatment response records.
CPAP data: Usage hours, pressure settings, AHI, and leak rates.
Decision-making tree: Based on periodic check-ups, symptoms, and static CPAP adherence data.
CPAP machine data: Basic adherence metrics (AHI, usage compliance, leak rates).
Limited clinical insights: Focuses on device performance rather than individual patient physiology.
Why we want Digital Biomarkers:
To enhance CPAP monitoring by integrating physiological responses into therapy tracking.
To develop smarter intervention strategies that adapt based on real-time biomarker deviations.
To optimize adherence support mechanisms using AI-driven insights rather than solely relying on static CPAP metrics.

Digital Biomarker Integration for New CPAP Patients (From Day 1)
38
📌 Goal: Apply digital biomarkers immediately to optimize therapy from the start.
Why Immediate Biomarker Use is Essential?
✔ New CPAP users often struggle with adherence.✔ Early monitoring detects therapy inefficacy before dropout occurs.✔ Identifying risk factors early allows for personalized intervention.

Adaptive Digital Biomarker Integration for Mid-Treatment CPAP Patients (3+ Months)
39
📌 Goal: Introduce biomarkers selectively based on therapy response and risk factors.📌 Purpose: Refine interventions dynamically and ensure long-term adherence.

Why Adjust Biomarkers for Patients Already in Therapy?

✔ Some patients show therapy response notstable (AHI improvement but persistent symptoms).✔ Cardiovascular markers may worsen over time, requiring additional tracking.✔ CPAP usage compliance ≠ therapy success (patients may still experience symptoms).
Refines CPAP therapy continuously for long-term adherence.

Detects therapy inefficacy and need for adjustments early.

Prevents unnecessary monitoring burden by targeting specific patient needs.

📌 Objective: Establish IT infrastructure, integrate data pipelines

Define patient inclusion/exclusion criteria for the pilot study.
Secure regulatory approval for patient monitoring and data collection.
Collaborate with Linde, IT teams, and physicians for data standardization.

🔹 Phase 2: Wearable Device Deployment & Data Collection (Weeks 6–16)
📌 Objective: Deploy biometric wearables for continuous physiological monitoring and collect real-time patient data.

Select wearable sensors: HRV, SpO₂, BP, EEG, respiratory effort sensors.
Train patients and caregivers on proper device usage.
Ensure real-time data transmission and secure cloud storage.
Validate data accuracy, reliability, and calibration with CPAP usage data.

Patient compliance issues (some patients may forget or refuse to wear devices).
Connectivity & data transmission reliability (ensuring minimal data loss).
Variability in biomarker readings requires continuous AI model adjustment.

Ensuring clustering accuracy handling variability in patient responses.
Physician acceptance AI models must be explainable and clinically validated.
