Criteria for selection of Medical Survey
Patient Profile and Baseline Characteristics
Criteria: Age, gender, BMI, health literacy, sociological factors, psychological history.
Rationale: Tailor surveys to individual characteristics, e.g., Beck Depression Inventory (BDI) for mental health needs impacting adherence.
·  Adherence and Usage Patterns
Criteria: CPAP use frequency, duration, consistency, and challenges (e.g., leakage).
Rationale: Low adherence may prompt quick assessments like the Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS), while consistent users benefit from comprehensive surveys (SF-36, PSQI) for deeper insights.
·  Intervention Type and Intensity
Criteria: Intervention level (remote vs. in-person), clinical needs.
Rationale: Simple surveys like ESS for low-effort interventions; more detailed surveys (SF-36, condition-specific) for intensive or clinical interventions.
·  Duration and Follow-Up Needs
Criteria: Short- vs. long-term monitoring and patient engagement.
Rationale: Frequent early-stage follow-ups (PSQI, FSS) for adjustments; long-term tools (SF-36) at 3–6 month intervals for sustained monitoring.
·  Targeted Health Outcomes
Criteria: Goals like improved daytime function or mental health stabilization.
Rationale: Mental health goals use BDI or SF-36; daytime function improvements measured by ESS and FSS to monitor alertness and productivity.
